# 90-Day US Federal Government Contracting & SBA 8(a) Self-Study Plan
## Updated Edition — Integrated Frameworks for 8(a) Excellence

> **Version 2.0** — Now includes McKinsey 7-Step PBA, VA Acquisition Academy, DAU Small Business Programs, and Army Source Selection Supplement (AS3), mapped to 8(a) program lifecycle and FAR Part 19 competencies.

---

## Foundation: What You Need to Know

This plan covers the four pillars of federal contracting success: **Business Posturing, Fiscal Posturing, Contract Posturing, and Certification Paths (NCMA CFCM)**. The updated plan weaves four mission-critical frameworks into your 90-day journey — each one directly connected to how agencies evaluate proposals, score offers, and award contracts to 8(a) firms.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation & Business Readiness (Weeks 1-3)

### Week 1: Orientation & The Federal Landscape
- **Topic**: Introduction to Federal Contracting
- **Study**:
    - Read the full SBA 8(a) program guide ([link](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs/8a-business-development-program))
    - Identify your local APEX Accelerator office ([link](https://www.apexaccelerators.org))
    - **Action**: Register in SAM.gov (System for Award Management) if not already done. This is your foundational first step.
- **Deliverable**: A document summarizing the 8(a) eligibility criteria and a checklist of your business's current alignment against those criteria.

### Week 2: Business Posturing I – Corporate Readiness
- **Topic**: Structuring your business for federal work
- **Study**:
    - NAICS code selection (identify your primary and secondary codes)
    - DUNS / UEI (Unique Entity ID) requirements
    - Cage Code
- **Action**: Ensure your business is properly registered and its profile is optimized in SAM.gov.
- **Deliverable**: Updated SAM.gov and SBA profile.

### Week 3: Business Posturing II – Capabilities & Past Performance
- **Topic**: Building a strong federal capabilities statement
- **Study**:
    - How to write a federal capabilities statement (vs. commercial resume)
    - Understanding government evaluation criteria (Past Performance, Technical Approach, Price)
- **Action**: Draft a draft capabilities statement for your business.
- **Deliverable**: A polished, one-page federal capabilities statement.

## Phase 2: Fiscal & Contract Posturing (Weeks 4-7)

### Week 4: Fiscal Posturing I – Financial Readiness
- **Topic**: Government accounting standards
- **Study**:
    - FAR Part 31 (Contract Cost Principles and Procedures) – specifically, Allowable vs. Unallowable costs
    - DCAA (Defense Contract Audit Agency) compliance basics
    - Indirect rate structures (Overhead, G&A, Fringe)
- **Action**: Review your current chart of accounts. Identify areas that need adjustment for government compliance.
- **Deliverable**: A gap analysis of your current accounting vs. government requirements.

### Week 5: Fiscal Posturing II – Pricing & Proposals
- **Topic**: Developing government-compliant cost proposals
- **Study**:
    - Cost-plus, Fixed-price, and Time-and-materials contract types
    - Cost realism and price reasonableness analyses
    - How to build a compliant cost/price volume
- **Action**: Create a sample cost proposal for a mock RFP.
- **Deliverable**: A sample pricing model for a government contract.

### Week 6: Contract Posturing I – The FAR & Regulation
- **Topic**: Foundational Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) knowledge
- **Study**:
    - FAR Part 19 (Small Business Programs) ([link](https://www.acquisition.gov/far/part-19))
    - Key clauses in FAR (e.g., 52.219-1, Small Business Programs)
    - Simplified Acquisition Threshold (SAT)
- **Action**: Register for an APEX Accelerator counseling session.
- **Deliverable**: A summary of the top 10 FAR clauses relevant to your business.

### Week 7: Contract Posturing II – Set-Asides & 8(a) Specifics
- **Topic**: Mastering 8(a) and other set-aside vehicles
- **Study**:
    - Sole-source vs. competitive 8(a) awards
    - Mentor-Protege programs (SBA and DoD versions)
    - Joint Ventures and teaming agreements
    - The 8(a) annual review process and compliance
- **Action**: Research 8(a) contract opportunities on SAM.gov or beta.SAM.gov.
- **Deliverable**: A list of 5 potential 8(a) contract opportunities for your business.

## Phase 3: Advanced Framework Integration (Weeks 8-11)

### Week 8: McKinsey 7-Step PBA – Performance-Based Acquisition Mastery
- **Topic**: The government-endorsed acquisition framework
- **Study**:
    - Read the McKinsey 7-Step PBA guide ([link](https://webarchive.library.unt.edu/eot2008/20081104220426/http://www.acquisition.gov/comp/seven_steps/about-project.html))
    - The seven steps: Establish the team → Describe the problem → Examine industry trends → Set performance metrics → Develop the strategy → Reduce risk → Implement & monitor
    - How PBA shifts from prescriptive requirements to outcome-based statements of objectives (SOO)
- **Connection to 8(a) & FAR Part 19**: 
    - FAR 19.502-2 authorizes sole-source 8(a) awards; understanding PBA makes your proposals responsive to how agencies actually evaluate outcomes
    - PBA aligns with 8(a) competitive advantage: smaller, agile firms can demonstrate superior performance metrics
- **Action**: Map each of the 7 steps to a scenario where your 8(a) business would respond to a PBA-style RFP.
- **Milestone**: Create a "7-Step PBA Response Template" — a reusable framework for analyzing PBA requirements and crafting compliant, competitive proposals.
- **Deliverable**: A written case study showing how you would apply PBA principles to win a competitive 8(a) set-aside.

### Week 9: VA Acquisition Academy (VAAA) – Customer Mindset & Competitor Intelligence
- **Topic**: Understanding how the VA trains its contracting workforce
- **Study**:
    - Explore the VA Acquisition Academy ([link](https://www.acquisitionacademy.va.gov/))
    - FAC-C certification tracks and what VA contracting officers must know
    - VA-specific procurement priorities: healthcare IT, construction, supply chain, professional services
    - The VA Acquisition Central portal and how the VA posts opportunities
- **Connection to 8(a) & FAR Part 19**:
    - FAR 19.203 sets VA small business goals; knowing their training helps you preempt evaluation criteria
    - VAAA-trained contracting officers evaluate proposals using VA-specific scorecards — learning their curriculum gives you insider advantage
    - 8(a) firms targeting VA must speak the language of VAAA-trained evaluators
- **Action**: Identify 3 VA-specific contract opportunities on SAM.gov that match your NAICS codes.
- **Milestone**: Complete the VAAA public-facing orientation materials and document the top 5 evaluation criteria VA contracting officers use.
- **Deliverable**: A "VA Customer Profile" document summarizing their procurement culture, training standards, and how to position your 8(a) business as a preferred vendor.

### Week 10: DAU Small Business Track – Speaking DoD's Language
- **Topic**: Learning how DoD Small Business Professionals are trained
- **Study**:
    - Review DAU SBP 1010 course materials ([link](https://www.dau.edu/blogs/dau-deploys-sbp-1010-introduction-small-business-programs-part))
    - DoD Small Business Strategy and goals (25% prime contract goal)
    - Market research methodologies: how DoD finds small business sources
    - Program mechanics: Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR), Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR)
- **Connection to 8(a) & FAR Part 19**:
    - FAR Part 19 implements DoD Directive 5124.02 requiring small business participation; DAU SBP 1010 is how DoD personnel learn to enforce it
    - Understanding DoD market research means you can position yourself before RFPs are ever drafted
    - 8(a) is a DoD priority program; SBP 1010 trains officials on how to justify 8(a) set-asides
- **Action**: Research DoD 8(a) spending data on USASpending.gov. Identify top contracting commands and their small business spending.
- **Milestone**: Draft a "DoD Small Business Readiness Checklist" based on SBP 1010 competencies.
- **Deliverable**: A market analysis showing which DoD agencies spend the most on 8(a) contracts in your NAICS codes, with contact information for their small business specialists.

### Week 11: Army Source Selection Supplement (AS3) – The Grading Rubric
- **Topic**: Mastering the Army's best-value source selection process
- **Study**:
    - Read the AS3 in full ([link](https://origin-www.acquisition.gov/node/63332/printable/print))
    - Mandatory DoD Source Selection Procedures (DoD Source Selection Guide)
    - Best Value Continuum: LPTA vs. Tradeoff methodologies
    - Evaluation factors, scoring, and rating adjectivals (Outstanding, Good, Acceptable, Marginal, Unsatisfactory)
    - Competitive range determination and discussions
- **Connection to 8(a) & FAR Part 19**:
    - FAR 19.804-6 governs 8(a) sole-source procedures; AS3 governs how Army evaluates any offer above $10M, including 8(a) competitive awards
    - AS3 scoring is the language Army source selection authorities (SSA) speak — your proposals are evaluated against it
    - Understanding AS3 evaluation factors (Technical, Past Performance, Cost/Price) lets you design proposals that score "Outstanding"
- **Action**: Deconstruct a recent Army RFP using AS3 evaluation criteria. Score your own mock proposal against the rubric.
- **Milestone**: Create an "AS3 Self-Assessment Scorecard" — a reusable tool for scoring your proposals before submission.
- **Deliverable**: A mock proposal debrief showing how you would address each AS3 evaluation factor for an Army 8(a) set-aside opportunity.

## Key Certifications Path: NCMA CFCM

To become a **Certified Federal Contracts Manager (CFCM)**:

1.  **Join NCMA**: Annual membership is required.
2.  **Meet Eligibility**: You need a combination of education and experience (specifics change, so verify on the NCMA site).
3.  **Study the CMBOK**: This is the exam blueprint.
4.  **Pass the Exam**: The exam tests your knowledge of the entire federal contract lifecycle, with a heavy focus on the FAR.
5.  **Maintain Certification**: Through continuing professional education (CPE).

**Why the CFCM?** It is the industry-recognized standard that proves you understand the FAR and the nuances of federal contract management, making you invaluable to any government contractor, especially one navigating the 8(a) program.
